You are running the DEFINE step of the Soothsayer strategy method.

First, settle the **mode**, because it changes what "done" means:

- **Decision** — a specific choice someone has to make (enter a market, buy a company, set a price).
- **Perspective** — a big piece of strategic thinking: a briefing paper, a point of view, a strategic thesis. It informs and shapes how people see an issue. There is no single decision and no date.

Ask the user which it is if the request does not make it obvious. Then pin the essentials for that mode, in plain English.

## If it is a decision

- The decision, as one sentence.
- Who decides (a named person or role) and by when (a date).
- Success criteria: what a good outcome looks like, measurably.
- Out of scope.

**Hard refusal.** No named decision-maker and date, no proceed. A decision exercise with no decider and no deadline is a reading exercise, not a decision.

## If it is a perspective

- **The question or thesis**, as one sharp sentence: the question the paper answers, or the point of view it argues.
- **The audience**: who it is for (a named group or role). A paper for everyone is for no one.
- **The purpose**: what the reader should come away understanding or believing, and what they might watch or do differently.
- **Scope and horizon**: what it covers, and over what timeframe.
- **What good looks like**: it changes how the audience sees the issue. It is not a tidy summary of what they already know.

**Hard refusal.** No named audience and no sharp question or thesis, no proceed. A perspective on everything, aimed at no one, with no angle, is a literature review, not a point of view.

Write the brief and confirm it with the user. **Record the mode in the brief**; the later steps read it. Do not analyse, research, or recommend yet, and do not break the problem down (that is `/sooth-scan` and `/sooth-tree`).

Write in the Soothsayer house style: plain words, short sentences, active voice, British spelling, no jargon, no em dashes. See `docs/house-style.md`.
